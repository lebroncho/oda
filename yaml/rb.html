Please try the following steps to resolve your issue.

  1. Clean dirt or debris that may obstruct the vents or diaphragm of your mic.
  2. Ensure the audio ports and jacks are clean and the headset mic is properly attached.
  3. Make sure the mic diaphragm is in front and the air vent is facing away from your mouth.
  4. Get the latest Windows update.
  5. Make sure your apps can access the mic.
  6. Ensure your headset is set as the default recording device.
  7. Set Windows microphone level and microphone boost to values that works well for your setup.
  8. Update the firmware on your headset if it is on the list.

Please try these final steps to resolve your issue.

  1. Download and run the Pairing Utility of your headset. If your device is not listed, skip this step.
  2. Check if a software is causing the issue by closing all Razer apps and software.
  3. If closing Razer Synapse fixed the issue, reinstall your headset drivers, including Razer Synapse, Razer 7.1 Surround Sound or THX Spatial Audio. Restart your PC then test your headset.
