Solutions

Important: You must attempt and check at least four (4) solutions below to contact us for support.

  • Power is important. Make sure your controller is fully charged.

    1. If the controller won't power on at all, place the controller onto the charging stand.

    2. Make sure the stand is plugged into a power outlet and is receiving power.

    3. Charge for at least 3 hours to fully recharge the battery pack, then try powering on again.

  • Connect your controller to the console with a USB cable.

    1. Use a USB-C to USB-A cable to connect the controller to your gaming console.

    2. Plug the USB-C end into the top of the controller.

    3. Plug the USB-A end into the USB slot on the front or back of the console.

    4. The cable will provide both power and a wired connection to the controller.

  • Update your controller through the gaming console's settings/dashboard (check console's user guide).

    • Remove all accessories/devices connected to the console when updating firmware.

  • Power cycle your gaming console (check console's user guide).

  • Press the controller's gaming console's wireless/pairing button for 3 seconds and release.

  • Turn Bluetooth ON and scan for nearby available Bluetooth devices.

  • From the list of devices found, select your gaming console wireless controller.

  • Connect and test your Razer controller on a different gaming console or PC.

Note: We may need a picture/video of the issue. Please have this before you contact our support team.
